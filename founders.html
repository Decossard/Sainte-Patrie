What Endures.

Sainte-Patrie is more than a city — it’s a chance to prove that Haiti can design and deliver a lawful, safe, and functioning urban community.

Led by Haitians and open to contributors from all backgrounds, this initiative calls on those who see nation-building as long-term legacy work. This invitation is for those ready to act before success is guaranteed. Founding institutions are not donors or spectators — they are builders of credibility, capability, and alignment during the city’s formative stage.

Who We’re Looking For

We’re seeking institutions, and individuals acting through institutions, with the capacity to turn vision into structure — across legal, financial, institutional, diplomatic, or cultural domains.

Whether a contribution is visible or discreet, what matters is that it moves the project forward in a real, measurable way.

Institutions may include foundations, companies, universities, associations, diaspora organizations, or other legally constituted entities.

What It Means to Be a Founding Institution

Recognition as a Founding Institution is not transactional — it is earned.

Institutions that make meaningful contributions during the city’s formative stage may be recognized as Founding Institutions of Sainte-Patrie. Their contribution is recorded in the Founding Institutions Registry, the public historical record documenting those who helped establish the commune.

Recognition acknowledges the role an institution played in helping bring Sainte-Patrie into existence. It does not confer governing authority, operational involvement, or privileged influence within the commune’s institutions.

Note: Being selected for consideration does not guarantee recognition. Contributions will be carefully evaluated for relevance and impact. Recognition as a Founding Institution will be granted only after formal review once the commune is established.

Recognition, Not Reward

Institutions formally recognized for their contribution may receive forms of civic recognition acknowledging their role in the founding of Sainte-Patrie. These may include:

  • Inclusion in the Founding Institutions Registry
  • Inscription on the Founding Wall within the city
  • Archival documentation of the institution’s contribution
  • Invitations to civic commemorations and major milestones related to the founding of Sainte-Patrie

These recognitions exist to acknowledge contribution and preserve the history of the city’s creation. They do not confer governing authority, operational involvement, or privileged access to public resources.

A Legacy Beyond Land

This is not about acquiring assets. It’s about helping shape a public city that functions — and creating a precedent for what Haiti can demand of its future.

Cities rarely emerge through government action alone. They take form through the efforts of individuals and institutions willing to contribute before success is certain. Those who participate in the founding phase help define the conditions under which the city will grow for decades to come.

Know the Risk

Sainte-Patrie is ambitious — and Haiti is a difficult terrain. Like any bold undertaking, this could fail. If that possibility makes an institution hesitate, this may not be the right opportunity. But if an institution moves forward knowing the risk, then it is the kind of partner this call is meant for.

How to Be Considered

To express interest, send a short letter of intent to founders@sainte-patrie.com explaining:

  • The institution
  • What it can contribute
  • What it has done, if applicable
  • Why this mission speaks to it

Supporting materials such as institutional profiles, references, or project proposals may also be included. Only selected institutions will be contacted.
